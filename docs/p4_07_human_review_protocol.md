# P4-G7: Human Expert Review Protocol

## Overview

This document describes the protocol for expert review of reaction candidate structures generated by the PC-CNG (Positive-Constrained Negative Generation) pipeline. The goal is to validate the structural validity, mechanistic plausibility, and competing pathway value of PC-CNG candidates compared to baseline methods.

**Trae's role**: Prepare materials, analyze results. Trae does NOT score candidates or fill in expert forms.

## Reviewer Requirements

- **Pilot**: At least 2 chemistry experts (PhD-level or equivalent experience)
- **Main review**: 2-3 chemistry experts
- Expertise in organic synthesis, reaction mechanism analysis
- No prior exposure to PC-CNG method or candidate sources

## Double-Blind Procedure

1. Reviewers receive forms with **only**: blinded ID + reaction SMILES (atom mapping stripped)
2. Reviewers do NOT see: candidate source, FNR risk score, model prediction score, stratum
3. Each reviewer scores independently — no discussion during scoring
4. Each reviewer sees a **different randomized order** of samples
5. Unblinding occurs only after all reviewers submit their forms

## Scoring Dimensions

Rate each candidate on a **1-5 Likert scale** (1 = strongly disagree, 5 = strongly agree):

| Dimension | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| **structural_validity** | The proposed structure is chemically valid (no impossible bonds, valence violations, or unstable intermediates) |
| **mechanistic_plausibility** | The reaction pathway leading to this product is mechanistically plausible given standard organic chemistry principles |
| **plausible_competing_outcome** | This product represents a plausible competing outcome (i.e., a reasonable alternative product that could form under the reaction conditions) |
| **likely_low_yield_failure** | This product is likely to result in low yield or reaction failure (i.e., an undesirable outcome) |
| **likely_feasible_positive** | This product represents a feasible positive outcome (i.e., a desirable product with reasonable expected yield) |
| **confidence** | How confident are you in your assessments above? (1 = very uncertain, 5 = very confident) |

## Reason Codes

Select one or more reason codes that explain your assessment:

| Code | Description |
|------|-------------|
| wrong_reaction_center | The reaction occurs at an unexpected or incorrect atom position |
| unlikely_bond_change | The proposed bond formation/breaking is unlikely under these conditions |
| condition_mismatch | The reaction conditions are incompatible with the proposed transformation |
| chemoselectivity_issue | Multiple reactive sites present; selectivity is problematic |
| regioselectivity_issue | Multiple regiochemical outcomes possible; prediction is questionable |
| stereochemistry_issue | Stereochemical outcome is unclear or incorrect |
| plausible_side_product | This could be a plausible side product rather than the main product |
| likely_feasible_alternative | This represents a feasible alternative pathway |
| insufficient_information | Not enough information to make a confident assessment |
| other | Other reason (specify in notes) |

## Control Samples

Each review includes:
- **Known positive controls** (gold): These are experimentally verified successful reactions. Experts should rate these high on structural_validity, mechanistic_plausibility, and likely_feasible_positive.
- **Known negative controls** (real negatives): These are experimentally verified failed reactions. Experts should rate these high on likely_low_yield_failure.

Controls are used to validate that the expert scale is meaningful (i.e., experts can discriminate positive from negative).

## Statistical Analysis

After all expert forms are returned, the following analyses will be performed:

1. **Human-human weighted Cohen's kappa** (pairwise between reviewers)
2. **Krippendorff's alpha** (multi-rater ordinal reliability)
3. **Control discrimination** (Mann-Whitney U: positive vs negative controls)
4. **Source-level effect size** (Kruskal-Wallis + pairwise Mann-Whitney with Cliff's delta)
5. **Reviewer effect** (Kruskal-Wallis for systematic reviewer differences)
6. **Confidence sensitivity** (Spearman correlation: confidence vs score extremity)
7. **LLM-human agreement** (supplementary only — CANNOT replace human-human agreement)

## Go/No-Go Criteria

### GO
- Human-human weighted kappa or Krippendorff's alpha ≥ 0.5
- Positive/negative controls significantly discriminated (p < 0.05)
- PC-CNG outperforms random/template on mechanistic_plausibility or plausible_competing_outcome (p < 0.05, positive Cliff's delta)
- Analysis considers reaction family and reviewer effects

### PARTIAL_GO
- Moderate agreement (0.3 ≤ kappa/alpha < 0.5)
- PC-CNG shows trend vs baseline (not statistically significant)
- Paper uses qualitative or auxiliary evidence only

### DEFERRED
- No real human review completed
- Cannot use "expert validated" claim
- Top journal sprint conditions not met

### NO_GO
- Agreement below 0.3
- Controls not discriminated
- PC-CNG not different from random

## DFT/xTB Subset

Computational validation (DFT or xTB) will be performed ONLY on:
- High expert disagreement cases
- High LLM-human disagreement cases
- High model confidence but low expert confidence cases

Computational results will NOT be treated as absolute ground truth.

## Instructions for Reviewers

1. Open your assigned form file (`reviewer_1.csv` or `reviewer_2.csv`)
2. For each row (sample), examine the reaction SMILES
3. Rate each of the 6 dimensions on a 1-5 scale
4. Select one or more reason codes (comma-separated)
5. Add any notes in the notes column
6. Save the completed form and return it
7. Do NOT discuss your ratings with other reviewers until all forms are submitted
8. If you are unsure about a structure, use the "insufficient_information" reason code and lower your confidence rating
